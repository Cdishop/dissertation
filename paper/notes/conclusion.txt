
# Conclusion

Cooperative behavior in the workplace has captured the attention of both scientists and practitioners as it can have both positive and negative consequences for individuals and collectives. Recently, researchers have set out to exlain what leads to sustained, long-run helping, a phenomenon captured by such phrases as "extra miler" and "good soldier." A majority of the antecedents examined so far are individual characteristics, such as affect, personality, motives, and fairness perceptions.
